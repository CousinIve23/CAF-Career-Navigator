## STAGE 1 — THE APPLICATION PROCESS
### "I just applied / I'm thinking about applying — what happens next?"

**The honest timeline:**
Most people expect 3-5 months. Reality: average is closer to 250 days (about 8 months). Silence does not mean rejection. You have NOT been rejected unless you receive a formal written rejection.

**Step by step:**
1. **Application submitted** → Confirmation email. Wait 2-6 weeks. Check email daily including spam.
2. **File review** → Basic eligibility check. 2-8 weeks. Respond to any communication within 24 hours.
3. **CFAT (Canadian Forces Aptitude Test)** → Cognitive test. Practice online first. Can retake after a waiting period.
4. **Medical examination** → Bring all records. The CAF updated its Common Enrolment Medical Standard in 2024 — asthma, anxiety, ADHD, allergies now accepted in many cases.
5. **Security screening** → Managed by DND's Personnel Security Screening Program. The longest step: 3-12 months. A large proportion of forms are returned for errors. Fill every field. Every address for 10 years, every employer, every country visited.
6. **Interview** → Be honest. Know why you want to serve. Know your trade choices.
7. **Enrolment offer** → Specific trade and start date.
8. **BMQ** → Basic Military Qualification. Training begins.

**What to do when you haven't heard anything:**
- Under 60 days: Normal.
- 60-90 days: Email your recruiting centre with full name, DOB, and file number.
- Over 90 days: Call directly. Be polite. Be persistent.
- Use the Online Applicant Portal at forces.ca.

**What to do during the 8-month wait:**
1. Get physically ready — run 5 km, bodyweight exercises, carry weight
2. Get documents in order — every address for 10 years, every employer, SIN, passport
3. Research your trade choices on forces.ca
4. Get finances in order — automatic bill payments, small emergency fund
5. Prepare your family — limited phone for 10+ weeks. MFRC can help before enrolment.
6. Stay on top of your file — check the portal weekly

### ELIGIBILITY QUESTIONS

**Can I join with a criminal record?**
A record does not automatically disqualify. Each case assessed individually. Severity, recency, pardons/record suspensions all factor. Some offences are absolute bars (serious violent/sexual offences). For most minor or dated offences: apply and let screening assess. THE RULE: declare everything honestly. Concealing a record is far more likely to disqualify than the record itself. Consult your recruiter.

**Age, citizenship, and education requirements:**
- Age: 16 for Reserve (parental consent), 17 for Regular Force. No strict upper limit but must complete training and serve meaningfully.
- Citizenship: Canadian citizen or permanent resident. Some positions require citizenship.
- Education: NCMs: minimum Grade 10 for most trades, some require Grade 12. Officers: university degree (or RMC entry).

**Tattoos, piercings, and appearance:**
CAF significantly relaxed its appearance policy. Visible tattoos now generally permitted. Face/neck/scalp restrictions remain in certain contexts. One pair of small earrings in uniform. Natural hair textures accommodated. Many tattooed people assume they can't join — most can. Check current CANFORGEN on Dress Instructions.

**Vision requirements / corrective surgery:**
Glasses and contacts fine for most trades. Some trades require full colour vision or uncorrected acuity (pilots, maritime surface officers). CAF may fund PRK for certain operational trades. Always declare actual vision honestly.

**Army, Navy, or Air Force — how to choose:**
- Army: Land operations. Gagetown, Petawawa, Edmonton, Valcartier, Shilo. Field environments.
- Navy: Maritime operations. Esquimalt BC, Halifax NS. Sea rotations 4-8 months.
- Air Force: Aerospace, space, cyberspace. Bases across Canada. Many technical trades.
- Trade choice largely determines element — not the other way around.

**Probationary period:**
Since December 31, 2024, all new members serve a probationary period while security clearances are processed. Conduct and values assessed.

## STAGE 2 — BMQ (BASIC MILITARY QUALIFICATION)
### "What happens at Basic Training?"

**What is BMQ?**
Entry-level training every CAF member must complete. Reg Force: CAFLRS in Saint-Jean-sur-Richelieu, Quebec. ~10 weeks. Reserve: weekends and/or summer block.

**What to expect:**
- First 2-3 weeks are the hardest — sleep deprivation, exhaustion, homesickness are by design
- Phone access heavily restricted early on — tell your family
- Issued uniforms, equipment, service number
- Training: drill, weapons, first aid, field craft, CAF values, fitness
- FORCE test during BMQ

**The FORCE Test:**
Universal fitness standard. 4 components: sandbag lift (20 kg), intermittent loaded shuttles (20 kg over 20 m), 20-metre rushes, sandbag drag (20 kg). Minimum standard is achievable with basic fitness. Retaken annually.

**Injury during training:**
Injury does NOT mean automatic release. Assessed at base medical. May be placed on Duty with Limitations (DwL) — temporary physical restrictions. Extended recovery: rehab/holding platoon. Medical Employment Limitations (MELs) document restrictions. Don't hide injuries. Most recover and complete their course.

**Failing or wanting to quit:**
Voluntary release during BMQ is possible but strongly discouraged in first weeks. If you fail a component: may be recoursed (redo the failed portion). Talk to your Platoon Warrant before deciding. Training staff observation: the overwhelming majority who want to quit in week 1-2 are glad they stayed by week 6.

## STAGE 3 — TRADE TRAINING & FIRST UNIT

**After BMQ:**
Posted to a training establishment for your occupation-specific course (DP1). Duration: combat arms 3-4 months, technical trades 6-12+ months. You are paid throughout.

**Key locations:** CFB Borden (support/tech), CFB Gagetown (Army combat), CFB Esquimalt/Halifax (Navy).

**PAT platoon:** You may wait weeks or months for your course to start. This is normal. Use the time — get fit, study, volunteer. It gets noticed.

**After trade training:** Posted to your first operational unit for on-the-job training (OJT).

**Rank structure:**
- NCM: Private → Corporal (~4 yrs) → Master Corporal → Sergeant → Warrant Officer → MWO → CWO
- Officer: Officer Cadet → 2Lt → Lt → Capt → Maj → LCol → Col → General ranks
- Pte to Cpl is largely time-based. Above Corporal: merit-based through selection boards. PERs matter enormously.

## STAGE 4 — PAY & ALLOWANCES

**Approximate 2025-26 base pay (before allowances and Military Factor):**
- Private (Basic): ~$3,578/month ($42,936/year)
- Private (Trained): ~$4,560/month ($54,720/year)
- Corporal (~4 yrs): ~$5,472/month ($65,664/year)
- Officer Cadet: ~$2,982/month
- 2nd Lieutenant: ~$4,830/month
- Captain: ~$7,176/month ($86,112/year)

**Military Factor (August 2025):** Pensionable percentage added to base salary. Compensates for reduced freedoms, family separations, posting uncertainty. Applied retroactively to April 1, 2025.

**Key allowances:** Separation Expense (Sep Ex), CFHD, Critical Occupation Recruitment Allowance, Instructor Allowance.

**Pay problems:** Go to your Orderly Room immediately. Do not wait. Errors compound. Grievance deadline: 90 days per QR&O 7.09.

**Hidden benefits most members miss:**
- Free legal advice from JAG (wills, contracts, civilian legal questions)
- Free will preparation through base legal services
- SCAN seminars (pre-release career transition)
- Tax-free income in designated operational areas
- CANEX online ordering at military prices
- CF1 discounts (insurance, banking, retail)
- SISIP Financial free planning sessions

**Civilian employment while serving:**
- Reg Force: Need CO's written approval. Must not conflict with duties or bring discredit.
- Reserve Force: Civilian employment is legally protected. Employers cannot discriminate for Reserve service. Provincial Reservist Protection legislation exists.

**Reserve classes:**
- Class A: Part-time evening/weekend training
- Class B: Full-time temporary contract
- Class C: Full-time operational deployment
- Pay, benefits, and SISIP coverage differ significantly between classes.

## STAGE 5 — SISIP FINANCIAL PROTECTION

**What is SISIP?**
Service Income Security Insurance Plan (RARM in French). Two parts: Long Term Disability (LTD) — income replacement if you can't work — and SISIP Financial — planning and insurance services for CAF members.

**Coverage:**
- Regular Force: Automatic from enrolment. CFLTD premium on your pay statement.
- Reserve Class C and Class B over 180 days: Automatic.
- Reserve Class A / short Class B: Can purchase optional coverage.

**What it pays:** If totally disabled: 75% of pre-release salary until age 65. On or off duty. Administered by Manulife: 1-800-267-6681.

**First 30 days:** Confirm CFLTD premium on pay statement. Book free financial planning session. Keep records of all injuries from the start.

## STAGE 6 — CAREER PROGRESSION & COURSES

**Career courses for promotion:**
Promotion requires both trade qualifications AND leadership qualifications:
- **PLQ (Primary Leadership Qualification):** Required for promotion to Sergeant. Competitive, leadership-focused.
- **ILQ (Intermediate Leadership Qualification):** Required for Warrant Officer.
- **DP courses:** DP1 (trade qualification after BMQ), DP2/DP3 (advanced trade).
- Without required courses, you cannot be promoted regardless of time served or PER ratings.

**PERs (Performance Evaluation Reports):**
Annual performance record written by your supervisor. Above Corporal, PERs are THE primary factor in competitive promotion boards. Read your PER before signing. You have the right to add comments. Ask your supervisor what they're looking for before the assessment period ends. A career of strong PERs yields good outcomes.

**Career Manager:**
Career Managers (CMs) at NDHQ Ottawa match members to positions and manage postings. Assigned after initial trade qualification. Many members never contact their CM — this is a mistake. They can explain available positions, flag preferences, advise on courses and career path. Contact through unit admin or My Career portal (EMAA/SGPM).

**CF Memo writing:**
Standard internal communication tool. Format: Date, To:, From:, Subject:, numbered paragraphs, signature block. One subject per memo. Cite relevant QR&O, DAOD, or policy reference. Be clear and professional. Your admin section has the template.

**Bilingualism and promotion:**
Second Language Profile (SLP/PSL): rated A (basic), B (intermediate), C (advanced) in reading, writing, and oral. Bilingual Bonus: monthly allowance for designated positions. Above Sergeant/Captain, many HQ and senior positions require a bilingual profile. CAF offers free Second Language Education (SLE) during work hours. Start early — get your profile before you need it.

## STAGE 7 — POSTINGS & THE POSTING PROCESS

**Mobility Allowance (effective April 1, 2026 — replaces old Posting Allowance):**
- Moves 1-3: $13,500 per move
- Moves 4-6: $20,250 per move
- Beyond 6: $27,000 per move

**CFHD (Canadian Forces Housing Differential):**
Helps offset high housing costs at posting locations. Based on local rental costs vs. percentage of pay. Updated every July. Lower ranks get proportionally more help.

**Annual Posting Season (APS):**
Most postings: spring messages for summer moves. Career Managers match members to positions. Submit preferences through My Career portal (EMAA/SGPM). Preferences are considered but not guaranteed. Posting messages typically give 4-6 months notice. Short-notice postings outside APS happen.

**When you receive a posting message:**
1. Contact receiving unit immediately
2. Contact MFRC at new base
3. Contact Housing Services Centre
4. Keep ALL receipts
5. Contact SISIP Financial to update plans

**Imposed Restriction (IR/RI):**
When posted but family can't/shouldn't move. IR pay covers accommodation at new location. Typically approved for up to 1 year, renewable. Apply early, document the reason clearly. Approved case-by-case — not a right.

**Service couples — co-location:**
CAF has a co-location policy. Both members must submit formal requests through respective CMs simultaneously. Considered but not guaranteed. At larger bases: usually works. When impossible: one member may request IR. Build relationships with both CMs early.

## STAGE 8 — DEPLOYMENTS

**Types:** Domestic (Op LENTUS, Op LASER), continental (NORAD), international (NATO, UN, coalition).

**Duration:** Typical international tour ~6 months.

**Pre-deployment:** Weeks of mission-specific training — rules of engagement, cultural training, first aid.

**During deployment:** Communication possible but can be restricted. Tempo varies.

**After returning:** Operational stress reactions are normal. Debrief with chain of command. Use health resources. Give yourself time to readjust.

**Family support during deployment:**
- MFRC deployment support programs (workshops, events, reintegration)
- Risk and hardship allowances on top of base salary
- SISIP Financial helps families manage changes
- 24/7 Family Information Line: 1-800-866-4546
- Peer support through MFRC is powerful
- Arrange powers of attorney and financial management before departure

**Tax-free deployment income:**
Members in designated operational areas may qualify for full income tax exemption. CRA publishes designated zones annually. Risk/hardship allowances also typically tax-free. Can represent thousands in savings per tour. Confirm with unit admin and SISIP Financial.

## STAGE 9 — HOUSING, BENEFITS & LEAVE

**Dental and vision:**
- Reg Force: Full dental at base clinic (free), vision care + prescription glasses covered.
- Dependents: PSHCP (dental/vision with limits).
- Reserve Class A: Limited.

**Parental and maternity leave:**
- Maternity: up to 18 weeks
- Parental: up to 63 weeks (standard) or 69 weeks (extended), shareable
- Top-up: CAF tops up EI to 93% of regular pay
- Adoption leave available
- Pension credits continue during leave
- One of the best parental packages in Canada.

**Annual leave:** 20 days/year to start, increasing with service. Plus statutory holidays.

**Buy vs. rent on the posting cycle:**
Posting every 2-4 years: real estate commissions (3-5%), legal fees, land transfer taxes = ~$15,000-22,000 per transaction on a $450K home. Two postings can consume all appreciation. BGRS/IRP covers relocation but not commission losses. Some members buy and keep as rental when posted. No single right answer. Consult SISIP Financial — they understand the posting cycle.

## STAGE 10 — PSP & BASE LIFE

**Personnel Support Programs (PSP):**
Run by CFMWS on every major base. Includes: fitness facilities (free/subsidized), sports programs, recreation, health promotion, CAF Fitness Virtual Training.

**Sports:** Visit cfmws.ca, select your base, or ask your section 2IC. Base leagues plus CAF-wide competitions and national championships.

**CF1 membership:** Free. Exclusive discounts with BMO, The Personal, CANEX, and more. Sign up immediately at cfmws.ca or the CF1 app.

**Education funding:**
- ILP: Up to $2,000/year for part-time studies
- ROTP: Full-time university paid for officer candidates (return-of-service obligation)
- UTPNCM: Full-time university for NCMs becoming officers
- ETB: Up to $85,764 for post-service education (6+ years of service)

## STAGE 11 — FAMILY SUPPORT (MFRC)

**Military Family Resource Centre (MFRC/CRFM):**
Free professional support at 25 of 27 CAF bases. Services: deployment support, counselling, child/youth programs, childcare connections, spouse employment, posting transition support.

**Use them early — not just in crisis.** New postings, deployments, kids in new schools, spouse job hunting. 24/7 Family Information Line: 1-800-866-4546.

**Childcare:** Subsidized spots — contact early (fill quickly). Budget 2024: $100M over 5 years for military family childcare.

**Moving with pets:**
BGRS/IRP: limited reimbursement for domestic pet transport (cats/dogs, ground). NOT typically covered: exotic animals, air transport, specialized boarding. Crown housing (PMQs): many allow pets with breed/number restrictions. International postings: research import rules 6+ months early.

## STAGE 12 — WHEN THINGS GO WRONG

**Pay/benefits wrong:**
1. Orderly Room (first stop always)
2. Chain of command
3. DCBA (formal adjudication)
4. Grievance process (submit within 90 days per QR&O 7.09)

**Harassment or misconduct:**
- Unit chain of command
- Sexual Misconduct Response Centre (SMRC): 1-844-750-1648 (confidential)
- Office of the NDA and CF Ombudsman (independent, when internal channels fail)

**Mental health:**
Access support through base medical clinic without career impact in most circumstances. Seeking help is strength. Base IPSC provides confidential support.

**Code of Service Discipline / Military Law:**
From enrolment: subject to the National Defence Act (NDA/LDN) 24/7. Can be charged for: Absent Without Leave (AWL/ASP), insubordination, drunkenness (even off-base), conduct unbecoming. Minor offences: Summary Trials. Serious: Court Martial. "I didn't know" is not a defence.

## STAGE 13 — CAREER CHANGES

**Occupational Transfer (OT/VOT/RC):**
Switch trades. Must meet CFAT score, medical/physical requirements for new trade, and generally have completed DP1. Process: express interest to CM; CO must support. Competitive. Resets trade qualification — you'll do trade training again.

**Component Transfer (Reg Force ↔ Reserve):**
- Reg → Reserve: Pension accrual stops at transfer. Significant financial implication. Consult SISIP.
- Reserve → Reg: Competitive, similar to new enrolment. Reservist experience is an advantage but not a guarantee.

**Commissioning from the Ranks (CFR):**
NCM to Officer. Programs: CFR Direct (NCMs with degree), UTPNCM (CAF pays university), CEOTP (various pathways). Rank often resets to 2Lt. A career change, not a promotion.

## STAGE 14 — LEAVING THE CAF & VETERAN BENEFITS

**Release types:**
- **Item 3b (Voluntary Release):** You initiated. Most common. Requires notice (typically up to 6 months).
- **Item 4c (Medical Release):** Medical conditions incompatible with service. Enhanced VAC benefits access.
- **Item 5 (5a-5f) (Compulsory Release):** Misconduct, imprisonment, service no longer required. Can affect pension/benefits.
- Release type matters — a 3b and 4c have very different benefit outcomes. Ensure correct item is applied. Consult JAG if questions.

**Voluntary release process:**
Talk to chain of command, then OR for paperwork. If ROTP or subsidized education: return-of-service obligation may apply. Do NOT simply stop showing up. CAF Transition Group helps plan next steps.

**Veteran benefits:**
- Register for My VAC Account before release
- CAF Transition Group supports through release process
- VAC disability benefits for service-related injuries
- ETB: up to $85,764 for post-service education (6+ years)
- Career Transition Services: job search, resume writing, interview prep
- VAC: 1-866-522-2122 or veterans.gc.ca
